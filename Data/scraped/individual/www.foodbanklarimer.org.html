The Need

Hunger’s Impact

For tens of thousands of households facing tough choices to make ends meet, cutting back on food is often the solution. But it comes at a steep price. For children, hunger impacts the ability to learn. For adults, the ability to work. For seniors, the ability to manage illness and maintain health.

Our Role

About Us

Food Bank for Larimer County is committed to not only providing the day-to-day nutritional assistance to our neighbors in need, but also to help stabilize their lives by providing health and hope, and putting them on a path to self-sufficiency.

The Future Need

Hunger in Our County

Using GIS Mapping technology, we have identified service gaps in our community. Using this data and population growth projections, the need will reach 12.2 million meals annually by 2035, an increase of nearly 64% from 2015.
