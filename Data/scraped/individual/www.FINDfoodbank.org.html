FOOD DISTIBUTION SITES ARE STILL OPEN

CORONAVIRUS:

FREE MOBILE MARKET DISTRIBUTIONS AND FOOD PANTRIES ARE STILL OPEN

 

UPDATED: 3/14/2020 8:00AM

FIND Food Bank has been working closely with County Supervisor Manny Perez’s office and Riverside County Dept. of Public Health to ensure that there is unity with all agencies to support our community with food assistance at this time.  We understand that many in our community are being hit hard with the economic impact Coronavirus has caused for many people in our Desert.  We are here for you and understand.

FIND’s Free Mobile Market community food distributions and FIND’s network of partner agency Food Pantries and community Feeding Sites will remain open. Click here to find site locations.  Please call the location to confirm time they are open.

PLEASE NOTE:  All of our sites are practicing Social Distancing techniques at this time.  Please make sure to abide by the site’s policies when you arrive to ensure that everyone has the ability to receive food quickly and with the safety precautions our County and State public health officials are requiring. These include:

  • Wash your hands regularly and use hand sanitizers prior to coming to the site and at the site if you have hand sanitizer with you.
  • Only 1 person from the household should attend the site to pick up food for your family. Please leave other members in the parking lot to wait for you when you come away from the food area.  They can  meet you then to help.
  • Leave children at home. Only bring children to the food site if you have no other child care options.
  • STAY HOME IF YOU ARE SHOWING ANY OF THESE SIGNS: Coughing, sneezing and running a fever.  You are able to have someone pick up food for you on your behalf and deliver it to you.
  • When standing in line to receive food provide at least 2 arms length of space between the person in front of you and behind you.
  • Step away from the line or outside if you need to cough or sneeze. Use your elbow to shield your face.

10th Annual TELETHON, April 3, 2020

FIND Food Bank needs your help.

The Coronavirus economic impact has taken a huge toll on our low income populations. This population is now growing with every requirement that employers must make including lay offs, severe cuts to employment hours, and hours of operations.  FIND projects serving now over 125,000 children, families, individuals and seniors every month who are in fear of where their next meal will come from. This is over a 35% increase just 2 weeks. Our clients are being forced to make choices between paying for their rent, medical bills or food.  FIND helps them not have to decide by providing them with food for free. We can only do this if we have your help.
